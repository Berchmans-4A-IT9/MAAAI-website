Mission

The organization “Mindanao Advocates Against AIDS, Inc” MAAAI is created to invest money to save lives, it mainly treats and helps people diagnosed with AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria. It's a global partnership with the government, society, corporate sector, and the ones affected in the community to establish an advocacy to carry out the three diseases and where they can train who also are willing to help out the sick. Knowing that these diseases, illnesses and sickness are compromising our health the MAAI wants to tell us one thing, which is to take initiative to make a turn to this problem and save lives. The institution’s goal is to provide better health services that are accessible for all citizens which is beneficial for the community to be holistically healthy.







History

The organization was officially launched as a treatment care and support
group on May 09, 2009 at the Episcopal Training Center in Matina, Davao
City under the care and support services of the Alliance Against AIDS in
Mindanao (ALAGAD Mindanao) with 16 founding members.
Programs undertaken by the organization focus on learning sessions, group
discussions, training and seminars/capacity building, consultations,
lobbying, advocacy, and networking. Moreover, care and support services
rendered by the organization incorporate peer/family counseling,
referrals, home-based and hospice care, and palliative Care.

Beneficiaries

According to the paper, the institution serves children between the ages
of 6 and 12. This includes more than 100 clients each month between the
ages of 13 and 19 and more than 800 clients per month for those who are
20 years of age and older. Some of these patients have tuberculosis and
malaria, and some are reported to have HIV/AIDS. These sick people seek
medical care from The Global Fund (GF) to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis, and
Malaria to receive treatment for their diseases.
