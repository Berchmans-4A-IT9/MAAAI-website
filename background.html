Problem Identification

The MAAAI has been unable to access HIV treatment due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Another issue the institution is dealing with is an increase in HIV cases and evidence of minors' early sexual encounters. The MAAAI Institution is said to provide free HIV/AIDS, Tuberculosis, and Malaria tests, which is why many individuals who cannot afford expensive hospital treatment come here instead. However, the pandemic affected the institution in such a way that they would have trouble accessing treatment for their patients, requiring our assistance. Our school is said to provide early access to treatment through online HIV outreach and teleconsultations, while also maintaining gender sensitivity and a non-stigmatizing environment for the partner community.










Problem Summary

Our project’s main objective is to show the patients at the partner institution that they belong to the community and are not societal outcasts. To fulfill this, we will be treating them with gifts, holding forums on their mental health, and engaging in online dialogues and socializing with HIV-positive patients to express our support for the struggles they are currently facing. To reach our goal, we will need a lot of support from everyone. In addition to accepting gift donations, we will also be buying more presents for the patients. Feel free to visit our website for more information, and any donations are highly appreciated.

Project Objectives

Our project’s main objective is to show the
patients at the partner institution that they
belong to the community and are not societal outcasts.
The specific objectives of our project include:
1. Conduct forums on their Mental Health.
2. Participate in Online Dialogues with HIV patients.
3. Treat the patients with gifts.



























Project Outcomes/Results

The project’s Outcomes/Results will be:
1. Low chances of HIV/AIDS cases around communities.
2. Decrease of Deaths relating to HIV/AIDS
3. Patients and healthy Civilians are all aware about HIV/AIDS










Project Outputs

The projects Outputs to fight HIV/AIDS are:
1. Conducted forums on their mental health.
2. Gave all patients their gifts.
3. Engaged in online dialogues and socializing with HIV-positive patients.

Success Indicators

The success indicators of our project is shown through the level of understanding of our participants from our forums, activities on the issue of HIV/AIDS. With the feedback and evaluation we get from our participants it shows concrete evidence that the accomplishment of desired objectives will be evaluated by participants through a “before-after” evaluation and the application of specific questionnaires which will assess participant’ knowledge and satisfaction with the organization. Such accomplishment of objectives will be evaluated by the same “before-after” evaluation. While the health strategies will be also evaluated according to their objective and action plans to know, what are the strengths and failures of such action plans and whether such health strategies were delivered appropriately. This enables us to demonstrate our program’s success or progress as with the information that was collected allows us to better communicate our program’s impact to others, which is critical for public relations, staff morale, and attracting and retaining support from current and potential funders.

Significance

The significance of our project proposal for the partner community's members is that it encourages them to remind their patients that they are not social outcasts because of their disease. As we all know, HIV is a virus that spreads through sexual contact and some people believe they aren't allowed to come into contact with them. With the online conversations, forums, and gift-giving we will offer, we can guarantee that the patients are reminded of how much they are loved and valued by everyone.
