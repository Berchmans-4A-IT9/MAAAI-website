What is the effect of using Daucus carota Peels (Carrot) compared to
Solanum tuberosum Peels (Potato) on the growth of the Solanum Lycopersicum Plant (Tomato)?

Problem Statement















Methodology


Independent Variable: Type of fertilizer (Potato and Carrot Peels)
Dependent Variable/s: The growth in height or mass of the tomato plant.
Controlled Variables: Type of plant (Tomato), type of fertilizer (Potato and Carrot Peels), type of soil, amount of water, type of pot

Procedures for Testing and Gathering/Obtaining Data

Producing or Sourcing the
Compost Material

1. After we peeled the potatoes, we could make another side
dish from the noble potato: potato peelings.
2. If you're not using the potatoes as compost, boil them with
the skins on, peeled, or peeled with the skins thrown into the
boiling water.
3. Once the potato peel water has cooled, pour it into a
watering can and water your plants.
4. Wash the carrot peelings to remove excess dirt before
chopping them into tiny bits.
5 Place the carrot slices in a container with water and
set aside for 24 hours in a cool, dry place away from direct
sunlight. The main idea here is to allow the potassium in
the carrot peels to be absorbed by the water.
6. Filter out the solid particles after 24 hours and use
the liquid to water or drench your plants.

Sourcing and Preparing the
Test Plants and Plant Soil

1. Prepare the 2 pots.
2. Plant the seeds.
3. Sprinkle in the carrot and potato peel water in each pot.
4. Water for 3 weeks.
5. Assess the results.
6. Declare which is better.













Making observations
and/or measurements
about the Compost

1. Examine the Plants.
2. Measure the plant growth rate weekly.
3. Declare which is better.

Dosage and Frequency of
Compost Application to
the Test Plant

1. Make sure that the compost is ready.
2. Test the compost if it's enough.
3. Freely put the carrot and potato peel
water around the plant in the pot.

Making observations
and/or measurements
about the Test Plants

1. Use a permanent marker to label each layer of the
pot and also mark the top of the soil level.
2. Once a week observe the composting pot and
take scientific notes to track the plant growth.
Make observations for three weeks.
3. After three weeks of observation and note-
taking, examine the compost by dumping the
contents out and examining the soil.
